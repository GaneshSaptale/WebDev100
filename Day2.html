What Is a Galaxy?

A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems, all held together by gravity.

A galaxy is a massive collection of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter that are held together by gravity. The word "galaxy" comes from the Greek word "galaxias," which means "milky," referring to the Milky Way galaxy that contains our Solar System. Galaxies come in different shapes and sizes, ranging from dwarfs with less than a hundred million stars to supergiants with one hundred trillion stars. The Milky Way, our home galaxy, is estimated to have around 100 billion stars. Galaxies are categorized based on their visual morphology as elliptical, spiral, or irregular. They are also thought to have supermassive black holes at their centers. The study of galaxies is an active area of research in astronomy and astrophysics.
